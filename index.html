Learn

How this rent vs buy calculator works

A good comparison is not just about your mortgage payment. It is about time horizon, equity, appreciation, rent growth, and what you do with the money that is not tied up in the home. If you are asking should I rent or buy, this page is meant to make that answer easier to understand.

You can also use this page like a rent vs mortgage calculator, but the broader value is that it includes taxes, insurance, maintenance, selling costs, and investing assumptions instead of stopping at the monthly payment alone.

What is a rent vs buy calculator?

A rent vs buy calculator compares the long-term financial impact of renting with the full cost of owning, then estimates how home equity and renter investments may grow over time. If you want to tighten the mortgage side, the Mortgage Calculator is a useful next step.

Why time horizon matters

Buying often looks worse over short periods because closing and selling costs are front-loaded. Over longer periods, appreciation and mortgage paydown have more time to help.

When buying may make sense

Buying can make more sense when you expect to stay put, can handle the upfront costs, and believe the home will build equity faster than renting plus investing.

When renting may be financially better

Renting can come out ahead when ownership costs are high, your expected stay is shorter, or the renter invests the difference instead of letting that cash sit idle.

Why investing the difference matters

If renting costs less each month, those savings can compound. In some cases, that investment growth can outweigh the equity built by buying. You can pressure-test that assumption with the Compound Interest Calculator.

A simple example

If renting saves $600 per month and that money is invested consistently, the renter path can build meaningful wealth over time. That is why a home does not automatically beat renting, even when it builds equity.

How to use the result

If buying only wins after your expected move date, renting may still be the better choice for now. If buying wins earlier and by a meaningful margin, ownership may be more attractive. The Net Worth Calculator can help place that housing decision inside your full financial picture.

Methodology

How this rent vs buy calculator estimates net worth

The model is directional, not a promise. Small changes to appreciation, investing returns, rent growth, or your time horizon can change the answer.

Buy path Mortgage costs plus ownership costs

The calculator includes mortgage payments, property taxes, insurance, maintenance, fees, closing costs, selling costs, and projected home appreciation.

Rent path Rent costs plus optional investing

The rent path includes rent, rent growth, renter insurance, moving costs, invested upfront capital, and optional investment of monthly savings.

Decision lens Net worth over your chosen time horizon

The key comparison is projected ending home equity versus projected renter investment value over the period you selected.

Sensitivity

What can flip the answer?

A rent vs mortgage comparison can change quickly when a few assumptions move. Time horizon, appreciation, investment return, and selling costs are often the biggest swing factors.

Timeline Staying longer usually helps buying

Short stays often favor renting because buying costs are front-loaded, while longer stays give appreciation and principal paydown more time to work.

Home growth Appreciation changes the equity story

If your home grows faster than expected, buying becomes stronger. If appreciation is weaker, renting may stay more competitive.

Investing Returns on the renter path matter

If renting leaves room to invest consistently and those returns are solid, the renter path can close the gap or pull ahead.

FAQ

Common questions

These quick answers support both first-time users and searchers looking for a plain-English rent-vs-buy explanation.

Is it better to rent or buy a home?

It depends on your timeline, local housing costs, down payment, appreciation expectations, and whether you invest the money you do not put into a home.

How does a rent vs buy calculator work?

It compares the full ongoing cost of buying with the cost of renting, then estimates how home equity and renter investments may grow over time.

Is renting cheaper than a mortgage?

Sometimes, but the better comparison is rent versus the full cost of ownership, including taxes, insurance, maintenance, fees, closing costs, and selling costs.

Does renting ever build more wealth than buying?

Yes. Renting can win when home costs are high, appreciation is modest, the stay is short, or the renter invests the difference consistently.

What is a break-even year?

The break-even year is the point when buying catches up to or overtakes renting on projected ending net worth under your current assumptions.

What assumptions matter most in a rent vs buy calculator?

The biggest drivers are usually your time horizon, home appreciation, rent growth, investment return, selling costs, and whether renting leaves enough room to invest the difference consistently.

Is a rent vs buy calculator accurate?

It is best used as a decision guide, not a promise. The answer depends on assumptions, and real-world appreciation, rent growth, and investment returns can differ from the estimates you use here.

Should I rent or buy if I might move soon?

If you may move in only a few years, renting often stays more competitive because buying costs such as closing costs and selling costs have less time to be offset by appreciation and mortgage paydown.

What is the difference between rent vs buy and rent vs mortgage?

A rent vs mortgage comparison usually focuses on monthly payments alone. A true rent vs buy calculator goes further by including taxes, insurance, maintenance, closing costs, selling costs, appreciation, and investing assumptions.
